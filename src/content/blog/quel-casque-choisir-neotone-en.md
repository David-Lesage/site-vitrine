The [Neotone](/le-neotone) has a dedicated headphone output — and headphones are [the most immersive experience](/blog/jouer-et-chanter-au-casque-neotone) it offers. But which ones should you choose? Here's my real setup, the one I use every day: **open-back headphones at home and in the studio**, **in-ears on stage**. Two uses, two tools, with prices and links.

## At home and in the studio: the Beyerdynamic DT-990 Pro

![Beyerdynamic DT-990 Pro headphones with grey velour pads](/images/prod-casque-dt990.webp)

My everyday headphones are the **Beyerdynamic DT-990 Pro 250 Ohm** (≈ **€148** — [see them at Thomann](https://www.thomann.fr/beyerdynamic_dt990pro.htm)). A studio reference for decades, with everything I love for handpan:

- **Open-back design**: the soundstage is wide and airy — the handpan's harmonics breathe, you're *inside* your bubble without feeling shut in.
- **Exceptional comfort** (velour pads): you can play for hours without fatigue.
- **Sturdy and repairable**: every part can be replaced; they'll last you years.

For the connection, two options depending on the session: **wired straight into the Neotone's headphone output**, or **into the output of my Boss RC-505 MKII looper** when I'm building loops — the headphones then give me the full mix (handpan + loops).

One technical note: this is the **250-ohm** version — simply push the headphone-output volume a little. Also worth knowing: *open-back* headphones leak a bit of sound — perfect at home or in the studio, less suited to shared silent places.

## On stage: in-ears, for discretion and efficiency

![KZ ZS12 Pro X in-ears, black metal shells and silver cable](/images/prod-kz-zs12.webp)

Live, big headphones no longer belong: too visible, too isolating from the audience, and the cable gets in the way. So I switch to **in-ear monitors** — more discreet, more efficient, and isolating just enough to hear my monitor mix cleanly.

Mine are the **KZ ZS12 Pro X** (≈ **€34** — [see them on AliExpress](https://fr.aliexpress.com/item/1005007219210466.html)). Don't be fooled by the price: they're **1DD + 5BA hybrid** in-ears (six drivers per ear!) with surprisingly precise sound and real bass — unbeatable value for stage monitoring.

## Wireless freedom on stage: X-vive U4

![X-vive U4 wireless system: bodypack receiver and XLR transmitter](/images/prod-xvive-u4.webp)

To cut the last cable between me and my monitor mix, I use the **X-vive U4** system (≈ **€199** — [see it at Woodbrass](https://www.woodbrass.com/ensembles-recepteurs-portables-x-vive-x-vive-u4-p378823.html)): the transmitter plugs into the output, the receiver clips to my belt, and my in-ears plug into it. The result: **I move freely on stage** with my monitor mix in my ears, and no trailing wire.

## In short: two setups for two moments

| Use | Gear | Price ≈ |
|---|---|---|
| Home & studio | Beyerdynamic DT-990 Pro 250 Ohm (wired, Neotone or RC-505 MKII looper headphone out) | €148 |
| Stage | KZ ZS12 Pro X in-ears | €34 |
| Stage, wireless | X-vive U4 system (+ the in-ears) | €199 |

Just starting out? Begin simply with the headphones you already own (a 6.35 mm jack adapter if needed): the joy of headphone playing is immediate. The day you step on stage, in-ears + wireless are life-changing.
