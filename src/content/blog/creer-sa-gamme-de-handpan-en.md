Creating your own handpan scale means choosing the set of notes that will make up your instrument — their order, their number and the emotion they carry. In the **Handpan Studio** app you can do it for free, draw your layout in colour, hear it live, then save it and share it with the community. This tutorial shows you how.

## What is a handpan scale?

A scale is the "character" of your pan: a central note (the **ding**) surrounded by notes laid out around the dome. Each scale has an emotional colour — meditative, bright, mysterious — depending on the intervals you choose. Understanding this is already composing: you don't place notes at random, you build a mood.

### Tonic, degrees and intervals

- **The tonic** gives the resting point, the "home" of the scale.
- **The degrees** are the notes built around it; in colours (ChromaKeys), each keeps a stable hue.
- **The intervals** — the gaps between notes — decide whether the scale sounds joyful, dark or contemplative.

## Create your scale in Handpan Studio (acoustic mode, free)

1. **Open acoustic mode.** The Discovery version of [Handpan Studio](/en/handpan-studio) is free and enough to build a scale.
2. **Choose your tonic.** It sets the overall colour and acts as the central note.
3. **Add your notes.** Place the degrees around the ding; the screen shows them in colour and draws chord Constellations in real time.
4. **Listen and adjust.** Play your layout, move or replace the notes that don't speak to you. Your ear decides, your eye confirms.
5. **Save your scale.** Store it to find it later, on an acoustic handpan or a [Neotone](/en/le-neotone).

### Lean on the colours

If you're a beginner, the visual method makes composing far more intuitive: choose the emotion first, then the colour, then the note. To grasp the principle, read [handpan by colours (ChromaKeys)](/en/blog/handpan-par-les-couleurs).

## Share your scale with the community

Once your scale is created, you can name it and share it: other players load it, try it and get inspired. It's also how you discover layouts imagined by others — a living atlas that grows with every creation.

Want to go further and be guided from the first note to your first composition? [David Lesage's lessons](/en/cours) walk you through it step by step.

Creating your own scale isn't reserved for experts: it's the most direct way to make **your** emotion sound, on your very own instrument.
