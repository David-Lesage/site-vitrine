The **Song Mode** in [Handpan Studio's](/handpan-studio) visual score editor shows lyrics with **chord diagrams anchored above the words** — like a guitar chord chart, but with colourful mini-handpans. You see where the chord changes, you sing, you play. No music theory required: the colours guide you.

## Sing and play at the same time

Accompanying a song on the handpan means juggling two things: following the lyrics and knowing which chord to play at which moment. Without a visual cue, you lose the thread the instant the melody gets tricky. Song Mode solves this at a glance: above each syllable where the chord changes, a **handpan diagram** appears. You read the line left to right, exactly as you'd sing it, and each diagram tells you precisely which notes to touch.

Several songs come ready with lyrics and chords: **Hallelujah**, **Let It Be**, No Woman No Cry, Stand By Me. You pick the title, the editor places the diagrams, and the song becomes playable.

### Example: Hallelujah

Open *Hallelujah* in Song Mode. Above the word where the first chord lands, a mini-handpan appears with its coloured notes. You play the chord, you sing the line, and when the next diagram arrives above another word, you change. The famous rise of "*Hallelujah…*" becomes a series of diagrams you chain in rhythm with the syllables. You don't have to count bars: the words carry you, the colours tell you where to go.

### Example: Let It Be

Same logic for *Let It Be*. The progression loops, and the diagrams return above the same words each chorus. In two or three passes, you memorise the path of the colours and sing while playing, without ever leaving the score. This is where [the handpan as an accompaniment instrument](/blog/accompagner-des-chansons-au-handpan) truly shines.

## How it works

It all starts from the app's **Export** button, which opens the visual score editor.

### Choose the song and display mode

Select a title from the library, then set the display to suit your need. The editor offers several modes: **Chord Sequence**, **Instrument only**, **Full**, and **Song Mode** (lyrics + diagrams). You also switch between **Portrait** and **Landscape** orientation depending on line length.

### Ink-Saver for printing

Before printing, turn on **Ink-Saver Mode**: the background goes white, ink savings are clear, and the score stays readable on paper. Perfect for placing a sheet on your stand next to the instrument.

### Export and keep

Once the display is set, you export as **PDF or PNG** — the diagrams can also go out as separate files in a ZIP. Every export discreetly carries the note "created with Handpan Studio by David Lesage". For everything about formats and export logic, the article on [keeping and sharing your chords](/blog/exporter-ses-partitions-handpan) gets straight to the point.

## Your song, within reach

Creating and exporting in acoustic mode is **free**; the Studio version adds cloud saving to find your songs everywhere. Open [Handpan Studio](/handpan-studio), load *Hallelujah* or *Let It Be* in Song Mode, and play your first song tonight.
